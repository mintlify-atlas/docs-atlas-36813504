---
title: 'Logging sets during an active KERN workout session'
description: 'Step-by-step guide to starting a session, entering weights and reps, reading last-session suggestions, using the rest timer, and saving your workout in KERN.'
---

The active workout screen is where your training happens. KERN shows you every exercise in your routine, pre-fills weight and rep fields with what you lifted last session, and automatically starts a rest timer after each completed set. When you are done, tapping **Finalizar** saves everything to your history and updates your dashboard stats.

## Starting a session

Tap the **Rendimiento** button on the dashboard, or select your routine from the home screen. The active workout screen loads your full exercise list with all sets ready to fill in.

## Logging a set

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your exercise in the list">
    The screen shows each exercise in your routine. Scroll to the exercise you want to log.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the 'Anterior' suggestion">
    Below the weight and rep fields you will see a suggestion like **"Anterior: 60 kg × 10"**. This is exactly what you lifted for that set in your previous session. Use it as your starting target.

    The fields are pre-filled with those values so you can either match them or type a new number straight away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your weight and reps">
    Tap the **kg** field and type the weight you are using. Tap the **reps** field and enter how many repetitions you completed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the checkmark to complete the set">
    Tap the checkmark icon next to the set to mark it as done. The rest timer starts automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rest, then repeat">
    Wait out the rest period — the timer counts down on screen. When it finishes, move on to the next set and repeat the process.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The suggested weight from your last session is a target, not a ceiling. If the previous weight felt easy, add 2.5–5 kg. Consistently beating your previous numbers is the core principle of progressive overload.
</Tip>

## How last-session suggestions work

Every time you load a workout, KERN fetches your most recent completed session for that routine and matches each set by its position. The values are shown as:

- **Anterior** — a text label (e.g., `"60 kg x 10"`) displayed below the input fields
- **prev_kilos** and **prev_reps** — the numeric values pre-filled into the input fields as a starting suggestion

If you have never completed this routine before, the fields start at 0 kg and 0 reps.

## The rest timer

The rest timer starts automatically after you mark a set complete. You can adjust the timer duration to match your preferred rest period. The timer is shown prominently on screen so you can keep track without watching the clock.

## Step counter

Your phone's step sensor runs in the background for the entire session as a foreground service. You do not need to do anything — KERN records your steps automatically and includes the total in your session summary.

<Note>
  The step counter continues working even if you briefly switch to another app. KERN uses a foreground service to prevent Android from stopping the count mid-session.
</Note>

## Finishing your workout

When you have completed all exercises, tap **Finalizar**. KERN will:

1. Calculate your **total volume** (sum of weight × reps across every completed set)
2. Display a session summary showing total volume and total steps
3. Save the session to your history immediately
4. Update your dashboard stats (workouts, steps, training days, and volume chart)

<Warning>
  If you close the app before tapping **Finalizar**, your session data will not be saved to your history. Always finish sessions using the button in the app.
</Warning>
